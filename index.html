SECTION 6 — STRATEGIC OBSERVATIONS Forward-looking considerations · operations · channel planning

SINGLE MENU LIVE — 2 MENUS RESOLVED

The consolidated WS + Retail menu is live as of July. The prior duplicate-APEX-account structure (separate CT/GA accounts, manual package splitting, and ERP re-sync per channel) is eliminated. Both channels now report from the same source of truth, filtered by Sales Rep (Chris Nava = WS, Spencer EJ Antrum = Retail) rather than reconstructed after the fact from store names — a cleaner, less error-prone split for weekly reporting.

H2 MONTHLY FORECAST — NEW WS TARGET SOURCE

Beginning July, the WS target column pulls from the H2 Monthly Forecast tab (CT WS Demand June-Dec 2026 V2) rather than the monthly Reforecast Summary used through June. This is a linear-ramp forecast (Jun accounts → Dec target) built off tiered velocity assumptions, and it already reflects account growth ramping through year-end. The original static S&OP baseline is retired for WS going forward — H2 Forecast is the sole active WS comparison line.

RETAIL TARGET UNCHANGED

Retail Demand targets remain sourced from the Supply & Demand Summary tab and are flat month-over-month (41,500 units total) — no revision has been made to the retail plan alongside the WS reforecast. If the flower-up / pre-roll-down demand signal from June holds through July, this may be worth revisiting so retail and WS targets stay directionally aligned.

FLOWER VARIETY THESIS — CARRY FORWARD FROM JUNE

June closed with 3.5g and 7g Flower both exceeding their prior RF Upside targets (152.5% and 155.1% respectively) while pre-roll categories underperformed on inventory that was available — a demand signal, not a supply constraint. Watch July's early weeks to confirm this pattern holds under the new H2 Forecast targets, which already build in higher flower volume.

CULTIVATION INVENTORY LAYER (SECTION 5)

As of the 7/1/26 snapshot, 7g Flower shows the tightest coverage (1,145 available units against a 13,353-unit combined monthly target — under 2 weeks of supply, flagged Understocked) while 1g Prerolls, 0.5g 7pk PRJs, and Vapes are all flagged Overstocked at well over 6 weeks of supply. This mirrors the June pattern: flower is supply-constrained relative to demand, other categories are not. Available figures intentionally include next-week reserved/pre-order units so cultivation stock isn't understated before those orders are actually fulfilled.

UNIT PRICE (AVG) NOW FORMULA-DRIVEN

Unit Price (avg) in Sections 1 and 2 auto-calculates as July Revenue ÷ July Units for each category, rather than being a manual input. For the week of 7/4, that produced $15.89/unit (wholesale) and $15.90/unit (retail) for 3.5g Flower — both above the $14.50 list price, reflecting the actual strain/tier mix delivered that week. July Revenue is a manual input meant to be reconciled against the CT Weekly Wholesale Updates sheet each week; update it there first, then carry the same figure in here.
