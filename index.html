SECTION 6 — STRATEGIC OBSERVATIONS Forward-looking considerations · operations · channel planning

SINGLE MENU LIVE — 2 MENUS RESOLVED

The consolidated WS + Retail menu is live as of July. The prior duplicate-APEX-account structure (separate CT/GA accounts, manual package splitting, and ERP re-sync per channel) is eliminated. Both channels now report from the same source of truth, filtered by Sales Rep (Chris Nava = WS, Spencer EJ Antrum = Retail) rather than reconstructed after the fact from store names — a cleaner, less error-prone split for weekly reporting.

H2 MONTHLY FORECAST — WS TARGET SOURCE (UPDATED 7/11)

The WS target column pulls from the (UPDATE) WS Forecast - H2 Monthly tab in the WS Copy of H2 Forecast (CT) sheet, a linear-ramp forecast (June baseline, fixed → December target, updated) built off tiered velocity assumptions. The July target was refreshed on 7/11/26 to 14,281 units. The original static S&OP baseline remains retired for WS — H2 Forecast is the sole active WS comparison line.

RETAIL TARGET UNCHANGED

Retail Demand targets remain sourced from the Supply & Demand Summary tab and are flat month-over-month (41,500 units total) — no revision has been made to the retail plan alongside the WS reforecast. If the flower-up / pre-roll-down demand signal from June holds through July, this may be worth revisiting so retail and WS targets stay directionally aligned.

FLOWER VARIETY THESIS — CARRY FORWARD FROM JUNE

June closed with 3.5g and 7g Flower both exceeding their prior RF Upside targets (152.5% and 155.1% respectively) while pre-roll categories underperformed on inventory that was available — a demand signal, not a supply constraint. Watch July's early weeks to confirm this pattern holds under the new H2 Forecast targets, which already build in higher flower volume.

CULTIVATION INVENTORY LAYER (SECTION 5)

7g Flower and AURA both show the tightest coverage (0.5 and 0.4 weeks of supply respectively, flagged Understocked) while 1g Prerolls, 0.5g 7pk PRJs, and Vapes remain Overstocked at well over 6 weeks of supply. This mirrors the June pattern: flower and AURA are supply-constrained relative to demand, other categories are not.

UNIT PRICE (AVG) NOW FORMULA-DRIVEN

Unit Price (avg) in Sections 1 and 2 auto-calculates as July Revenue ÷ July Units for each category, rather than being a manual input. For the week of 7/4, that produced $15.89/unit (wholesale) and $15.90/unit (retail) for 3.5g Flower — both above the $14.50 list price, reflecting the actual strain/tier mix delivered that week. July Revenue is a manual input reconciled against the CT Weekly Wholesale Updates sheet each week.

WEEK 2 (7/11) — CONFIRMED AND INCLUDED

Every line item in the 7/5-7/11 WS and Retail Product Sales exports showed Order Status "In-transit" or "Pending shipment" rather than "Delivered" — confirmed that these count for this week based on delivery dates (7/8-7/9) falling inside the window. WS AURA came in at 1,050 units (90.8% of its full-month H2 Forecast after 2 weeks) and Retail 0.5g 7pk PRJs jumped to 1,086 units from 300 the prior week.
